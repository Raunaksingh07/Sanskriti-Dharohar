Open Timings The Durgiana Temple in Amritsar is open to the public from 6:00 AM to 10:00 PM every day of the week, according to travel websites
Prayer Timings The Durgiana Temple in Amritsar is open daily from 6:00 AM to 10:00 PM. However, the timings can vary slightly depending on the season, weather, or special occasions. The temple also hosts aarti ceremonies twice a day, morning and evening, which are worth experiencing.
Pooja Items The Durgiana Temple, also known as Lakshmi Narayan Temple, requires typical Hindu puja items for worship. These include idols or deities, a puja thali, incense sticks, lamps (diyas or deepam), holy water, camphor, sandalwood paste, rice, flowers, and other essential ingredients like kumkum and cotton wicks, according to temple guidelines. Specific items like chuni or sari, roli, moli, and a coconut may also be used.
Other Deities Besides Goddess Durga, the primary deity, Durgiana Temple also houses idols of other prominent Hindu deities like Lakshmi Narayan (Vishnu and Lakshmi), Lord Rama, Sita, Lakshman, Bharat, Radha Krishna, Hanuman, and Lord Shiva. The temple complex also includes shrines dedicated to various other gods and goddesses, making it a diverse center of worship.
Amenities The Durgiana Temple in Amritsar offers several amenities for visitors, including a large parking area, a shopping complex, and an open-air theater. It also has a Yatri Niwas (pilgrim's inn) with modern amenities like AC, TVs, and bathrooms. Additionally, the temple complex includes a free kitchen (Langar), a sacred pond for taking holy dips, and various religious inscriptions and carvings on the silver doors
Festivals Holi, Diwali, Janmashtami, and Navratri
Marriage if happen A wedding at Durgiana Temple in Amritsar would be a religious ceremony conducted by a Hindu priest (Pandit) within the temple premises, following Hindu rituals and customs. The temple itself doesn't host weddings, but Hindu weddings can be performed there with the assistance of a Pandit who is knowledgeable in Hindu wedding ceremonies.
Food Provided by Temple A variety of bhog (offerings of food) to the deities throughout the day, including Mohan Thaal, Makhan Mishri, and Preeti Boj with items like roti, dal, rice, and sweets.

Durgiana Temple or Shri Durgiana Mandir is a Hindu temple situated in the city of Amritsar in Punjab, IndiaThough a Hindu temple, its architecture is similar to the Sikh Harmandir Sahib (Golden Temple). The temple derives its name from the Goddess Durga, the chief Goddess who is worshipped here. Murtis of Lakshmi and Vishnu are also located and worshipped in the temple. Although it is believed that Durgiana Mandir was built in 1921, the temple existed before it was rebuilt in 1921.[citation needed] It is confirmed by record in Amritsar District Gazetteer of 1893, which spoke about the Durgiana Sarovar and "Devi dwara" surrounding it which was visited by Hindu pilgrims.

Mythology

The Sri Durgiana temple is a very important temple for Hindus. In the courtyard of the temple is a tree where Lava and Kusha bound Hanuman after the captured challenge horse of the Ashvamedha yajna. Ikshvaku, the grandson of sun-god, performed a number of yagyas on this land.

History Of Mandir

The original temple was built in 16th century.[5][unreliable source?] The architecture of Sri Durgiana Temple is similar to Shri Harmandir Sahib. It was built in 1921 by Guru Harsai Mal Kapoor in the architectural style of the Sikh Shri Harmandir Sahib (Golden Temple). Harsai Mal was a descendant of Prithi Chand and hence Guru Ram Das. Location

The temple is situated near the Hathi gate in Amritsar. It is very close to the Amritsar railway station, and is about 1.5 kilometres (0.93 mi) from the bus station.
