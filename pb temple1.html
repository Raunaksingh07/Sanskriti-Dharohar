Golden Temple,Amritsar
Main Prasad The main prasad (holy offering) distributed at the Golden Temple in Amritsar is Karah Prasad.
Open Timings The Golden Temple in Amritsar is generally open 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. However, the main prayer ceremonies and the best times to visit are during the early morning hours and in the evening. The temple's doors open for devotees at 2:15 AM, with various rituals and prayers taking place throughout the day.
Prayer Timings beginning early in the morning.
Pooja Items he Golden Temple, also known as Harmandir Sahib, is a central place of worship for Sikhs and doesn't have specific "pooja items" in the way Hindu temples might. However, devotees often use items like incense, lamps (diyas), and prayer beads (mala) during their visit, and religious artifacts like miniature Golden Temple replicas are popular souvenirs. While the temple itself is made of marble and gold, it's the spiritual experience and religious practices within that are central to the Golden Temple's significance.
Other Deities The primary deity of the Golden Temple (Harmandir Sahib) in Amritsar is God (Waheguru), as enshrined in the Guru Granth Sahib.
Amenities The Golden Temple, also known as Sri Harmandir Sahib, in Amritsar, offers various amenities for visitors, including free food (langar), free shoe storage, and free cloakrooms for luggage.
Festivals Baisakhi, Guru Nanak Gurpurab, Diwali (also known as Bandi Chor Divas), and Hola Mohalla.
Marriage if happen Yes, Sikh couples can get married at the Golden Temple (Harmandir Sahib) in Amritsar through an Anand Karaj ceremony. The ceremony involves taking four rounds (Lavan) around the Guru Granth Sahib, accompanied by prayers and hymns. While the Golden Temple is an open place of worship for all, specific guidelines apply to Anand Karaj ceremonies, and it's advisable to check with the temple management for details.
Education if provided While not a formal school, the temple complex and its teachings provide valuable lessons on Sikhism, spirituality, and community
Food Provided by Temple The Golden Temple provides a free, vegetarian meal called langar.

Dimensions The Golden Temple, or Sri Harmandir Sahib, is built on a 67 sq ft platform and is surrounded by the Amrit Sarovar, a 150 sq meter lake. The main temple structure itself is 40.5 sq ft, with a length of 202 feet and a width of 21 feet.

The Golden Temple, also known as Sri Harmandir Sahib, is a prominent Sikh gurdwara located in Amritsar, Punjab, India. It is the holiest shrine in Sikhism and a major pilgrimage destination. The temple is renowned for its stunning golden dome and intricate architecture, surrounded by a sacred pool called the Amrit Sarovar.

Nomenclature

The Harmandir Sahib is also spelled as Harimandar or Harimandir Sahib.It is also called the Durbār Sahib, which means "sacred audience", as well as the Golden Temple for its gold leaf-covered sanctum centre.The word "Harmandir" is composed of two words: "Hari", which scholars translate as "God ",and "mandir", which means "house". "Sahib" is further appended to the shrine's name, the term often used within Sikh tradition to denote respect for places of religious significance. The Sikh tradition has several gurdwaras named "Harmandir Sahib", such as those in Kiratpur and Patna. Of these, the one in Amritsar is most revered

History

According to the Sikh historical records, the land that became Amritsar and houses the Harimandir Sahib was chosen by Guru Amar Das, the third Guru of the Sikh tradition. It was then called Guru Da Chakk, after he had asked his disciple Ram Das to find land to start a new town with a man-made pool as its central point.After Guru Ram Das succeeded Guru Amar Das in 1574, and in the face of hostile opposition from the sons of Amar Das,Ram Das founded the town that came to be known as "Ramdaspur". He started by completing the pool with the help of Baba Buddha (not to be confused with the Buddha of Buddhism). Ram Das built his new official centre and home next to it. He invited merchants and artisans from other parts of India to settle in the new town with him. A Sikh Guru (perhaps Guru Arjan) seated in the Golden Temple at Amritsar in the late 16th or early 17th century, 1830 Guler painting Ramdaspur town expanded during the time of Guru Arjan financed by donations and constructed by voluntary work. The town grew to become the city of Amritsar, and the area grew into the temple complex. The construction activity between 1574 and 1604 is described in Mahima Prakash Vartak, a semi-historical Sikh hagiography text likely composed in 1741, and the earliest known document dealing with the lives of all the ten Gurus.Arjan installed the scripture of Sikhism inside the new gurdwara in 1604.Continuing the efforts of Ram Das, Arjan established Amritsar as a primary Sikh pilgrimage destination. He wrote a voluminous amount of Sikh scripture including the popular Sukhmani Sahib.

Akal Takht and Teja Singh Samundri Hall

In front of the sanctum and the causeway is the Akal Takht building. It is the chief Takht, a centre of authority in Sikhism. Its name Akal Takht means "throne of the Timeless (God)". The institution was established by Guru Hargobind after the martyrdom of his father Guru Arjan, as a place to conduct ceremonial, spiritual and secular affairs, issuing binding writs on Sikh gurdwaras far from his own location. A building was later constructed over the Takht founded by Guru Hargobind, and this came to be known as Akal Bunga. The Akal Takht is also known as Takht Sri Akal Bunga. The Sikh tradition has five Takhts, all of which are major pilgrimage sites in Sikhism. These are in Anandpur, Patna, Nanded, Talwandi Sabo and Amritsar. The Akal Takht in the Golden Temple complex is the primary seat and chief.[84][85] It is also the headquarters of the main political party of the Indian state of Punjab, Shiromani Akali Dal (Supreme Akali Party).[4] The Akal Takht issues edicts or writs (hukam) on matters related to Sikhism and the solidarity of the Sikh community. The Teja Singh Samundri Hall is the office of the Shiromani Gurdwara Parbandhak Committee (Supreme Committee of Temple Management). It is located in a building near the Langar-kitchen and Assembly Hall. This office coordinates and oversees the operations of major Sikh temples.

Pilgrims

The Golden Temple, also known as Sri Harmandir Sahib, is the holiest shrine in Sikhism and a major pilgrimage site located in Amritsar, Punjab, India. It's a place of worship and a symbol of Sikh values like equality, service, and community. The temple is renowned for its stunning architecture, including its gold-plated exterior, and its serene location within the Amrit Sarovar (holy pool).
