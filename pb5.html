The Moorish Mosque is a mosque located in Kapurthala in the state of Punjab, India. Commissioned by Maharajah Jagatjit Singh, the last ruler of Kapurthala,[1] the mosque was completed in 1930 in a fusion of Indo-Islamic and Moorish Revival styles, patterned on the lines of the Grand Mosque of Marrakesh, Morocco.[citation needed] At the time of its completion, Kapurthala city was the capital city of the Kapurthala State, known as 'Mini Paris of Punjab' and the mosque was stated to be one of the best in South-east Asia. The mosque is a monument protected by the Punjab State Department of Archaeology.

The Khairuddin Mosque is a historically and culturally significant building, renowned for its unique architectural design. The mosque is built in an arcuate style, where the weight of the superstructure above doors and windows is carried by arches. This beautiful design is reminiscent of traditional Islamic architecture, with its exquisite arches and beams. The walls of the mosque are decorated with elaborate calligraphy Aayats. The mosque's white marble structure is adorned with tiny green paintings. Under the clear blue skies, the location appears mystical.

History Of masjid

The Khairuddin Masjid, also known as Jama Masjid, is a stunning mosque erected in 1876 CE by Muhammad Khairuddin. The mosque is crucial to India's quest for independence. From the pillars of this mosque, Syed Ata Ullah Shah Bukhari, a Muslim intellectual and political figure, urged the populace to fight against British rule. It was where Tootie-e-Hind, Shah Attaullah Bukhari, issued the famous proclamation to declare war against the plundering British. The Khairuddin Mosque is historically and culturally significant and well-known for its architecture.

Entry fee and timing to the Khairuddin Mosque, Amritsar

The Khairuddin Mosque does not charge any entrance fee. You can photograph it and capture its beauty without having to pay anything. The mosque is open every day from 6 am to 8 pm. But keep in mind that the mosque is off-limits to tourists during the namaz hours, so avoid going there during the namaaz time.
